## Why This Matters

Civil rights cases involving institutional misconduct—police violence, prosecutorial abuse, systemic discrimination—often go uninvestigated because legal research is expensive and slow. Evidence is scattered across public records, court filings, and local news archives. Many cases that could win never get to an attorney who could pursue them.

An autonomous legal research platform changes that. It can:
- Research patterns of misconduct across jurisdictions and years
- Identify evidence in public records at scale
- Prepare case analyses faster than any human paralegal
- Surface cases that are otherwise invisible to the legal system

But the platform's findings mean nothing without human judgment. You'll be the person who decides: Is this case viable? Is it safe to publish? Will it actually help? Does the evidence hold up? This is where judgment, institutional knowledge, and civil rights commitment matter most.
