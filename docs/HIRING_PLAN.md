## 2. Recruiting Channels & Outreach Strategy

### CTO / Executive Director (Leadership Roles)
- **Primary:** Warm outreach via existing networks (Founder's personal connections, angel investors, past colleagues)
- **Secondary:** YC jobs board, Lever, LinkedIn (executive search)
- **Tertiary:** Legal tech communities, civil rights org networks, startup accelerators
- **Budget:** Up to $20K recruitment fee if using retained search firm

### Engineering Roles (Backend, Platform, Agent/LLM)
- **Primary:** GitHub (code quality assessment + outreach), LinkedIn, referrals from CTO
- **Secondary:** YC jobs, Hacker News, tech-focused job boards
- **Tertiary:** Legal tech job boards, startup networks
- **Budget:** $0-5K per role (no agency fees; rely on CTO network + warm referrals)

### Program/Operations Roles (Research, Investigative, Comms)
- **Primary:** Legal job boards (NLADA, Public Interest Law Network), civil rights org networks
- **Secondary:** LinkedIn, nonprofit job boards (Idealist.org, Candid)
- **Tertiary:** Direct outreach to attorneys, investigators, advocacy organizations in MT/WA
- **Budget:** $0-3K per role

### Legal Advisor (Contract)
- **Primary:** Direct outreach to civil rights attorneys already engaged with MISA
- **Secondary:** Law school alumni networks, nonprofit legal networks
- **Tertiary:** Legal temp/contract agencies (if needed)
- **Budget:** Hourly rate $100-150; ~10 hrs/week contract

## 3. Interview Process by Role

### CTO / VP Engineering (90-minute total)
1. **Phone screen (30 min):** Culture fit, mission alignment, availability
2. **Technical deep-dive (90 min, with CTO or external tech leader):**
   - Architecture trade-off scenario (MCAS schema decisions)
   - Staffing/hiring philosophy
   - Security/compliance experience
3. **Final round (30 min, with Founder):** Mission, values, long-term vision

**Evaluation rubric:**
- Technical depth (backend/infra): 5/5
- Architecture judgment: 5/5
- Communication clarity: 4/5
- Team leadership: 4/5
- Mission alignment: 4/5
- **Bar:** ≥17/25 to advance; ≥20/25 to offer

---

### Executive Director, Civil Rights Programs (90 minutes)
1. **Phone screen (30 min):** Career trajectory, civil rights commitment, case judgment
2. **Program deep-dive (60 min, with Founder + external civil rights advisor):**
   - Case selection and prioritization
   - Attorney relationship building
   - Handling of sensitive allegations
   - Team leadership and culture
3. **Values check (30 min, with Founder):** Mission fit, institutional knowledge of civil rights work

**Evaluation rubric:**
- Civil rights expertise: 5/5
- Case judgment: 5/5
- Attorney relationships/networks: 4/5
- Team leadership: 4/5
- Mission commitment: 5/5
- **Bar:** ≥18/25 to advance; ≥21/25 to offer

---

### Backend/Platform/Agent/LLM Engineers (90 minutes total)
1. **Phone screen (30 min):** Background, relevant experience, timezone/logistics
2. **Technical coding/design (60 min, with CTO):**
   - Backend: REST API design, database modeling, testing strategy
   - Platform: Infrastructure trade-offs, deployment strategy, monitoring
   - Agent/LLM: Prompt engineering approach, agent design, quality metrics
3. **Culture fit (30 min, with CTO):** Team dynamics, learning, mission

**Evaluation rubric (all engineering roles):**
- Technical depth in domain: 5/5
- Code quality / system design: 5/5
- Problem-solving approach: 4/5
- Communication: 3/5
- Mission alignment: 3/5
- **Bar:** ≥15/25 to advance; ≥18/25 to offer

---

### Research Lead / Investigative Lead (60 minutes)
1. **Phone screen (30 min):** Background, case management experience, legal knowledge
2. **Domain expertise (30 min, with Executive Director):**
   - Research methodology (Research Lead)
   - FOIA management and public records (Investigative Lead)
   - Quality standards for legal work

**Evaluation rubric:**
- Domain expertise: 5/5
- Attention to detail: 4/5
- Attorney collaboration: 4/5
- **Bar:** ≥10/15 to advance; ≥12/15 to offer

## 5. Onboarding Plan

### Week 1 (All New Hires)
- Day 1: Equipment setup, systems access (Slack, GitHub, Google Workspace, MCAS)
- Day 2-3: Codebase tour (with their manager), architecture overview (SPEC.md, AGENTS.md)
- Day 3-4: Legal context (civil rights 1983 claims, MT/WA jurisdiction specifics)
- Day 4-5: Team dinners, culture introduction
- **Week 1 deliverable:** Employee understands the platform mission and architecture

### Weeks 2-4 (Role-Specific)

#### CTO/VP Engineering
- Week 2: Architecture review (prioritize MCAS, identify MVPs, defer non-critical components)
- Week 3: Engineering hiring kickoff (define job descriptions, begin outreach)
- Week 4: First weekly one-on-ones with team; quarterly planning
- **Month 1 deliverable:** Architecture review complete; MVP scope agreed with CEO

#### Executive Director
- Week 2: Attorney network meetings (identify partners for case referral)
- Week 3: Research methodology review (understand agent capabilities)
- Week 4: Case selection criteria definition (first case intake form)
- **Month 1 deliverable:** Attorney partnerships mapped; case selection criteria defined

#### Backend Engineer
- Week 2: MCAS schema deep-dive (understand access control, data model)
- Week 3: API design (REST endpoints for case management)
- Week 4: First tasks (implement case/matter CRUD endpoints)
- **Month 1 deliverable:** Basic case management API scaffolded

#### Research/Investigative Leads
- Week 2: Agent capability review (what Iris, Rae, Chronology can do)
- Week 3: Workflow documentation (case → research → output)
- Week 4: First test case setup (begin testing agent output)
- **Month 1 deliverable:** Research workflow documented; first test case in progress

## 8. Decision Gates & Contingencies

### If CTO hire slips beyond June 30
- **Contingency:** Interim technical advisor (contract CTO, 20 hrs/week) bridges to permanent hire
- **Impact:** Hiring timeline extends by 4-6 weeks; delay Backend Engineer hire to September

### If Executive Director hire slips beyond July 15
- **Contingency:** CEO leads case strategy directly until hire closes
- **Impact:** Attorney partnerships delayed; first case publication pushed to October

### If no suitable Backend Engineer by August 15
- **Contingency:** CTO implements MCAS MVP alone; defer Backend hire to October
- **Impact:** MCAS MVP ships late (October instead of September); impacts case research timeline

### If Engineering hiring falls short by EOY
- **Contingency:** Reassess scope; defer Agent/LLM and Comms roles to Q1 2027
- **Impact:** Platform launch delayed 3 months; adjust case publication timeline

## 9. Key Decisions for CEO

Before executing this plan:

1. **Publication authority:** Who approves published cases? CEO only, or can Executive Director approve with CEO visibility?
2. **Search firm:** Hire a retained search firm for leadership roles (CTO, Executive Director) or rely on warm network outreach?
3. **Compensation flexibility:** Can equity go above 1.0% for CTO if candidate negotiates? (Recommended: cap at 1.2%)
4. **Board approval:** Does board need to sign off on hiring budget before recruitment begins?
5. **Referral bonuses:** Should MISA offer $5K referral bonus for successful engineer hires? (Recommended: yes for Q2-Q3)
6. **Background checks:** Standard startup vetting or enhanced (for legal/sensitive work)? (Recommended: enhanced)
