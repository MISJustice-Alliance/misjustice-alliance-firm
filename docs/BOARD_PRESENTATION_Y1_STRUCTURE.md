## Organizational Model: Hybrid AI-First + Human Judgment

### The Problem We're Solving

Civil rights cases involving institutional misconduct (police violence, prosecutorial abuse) often go uninvestigated because:
- Legal research is slow and expensive
- Evidence is scattered across public records
- Many potential cases never reach an attorney

### Our Solution

A hybrid team that separates work by what humans and AI do best:

**Agents own:**
- Autonomous investigation (Iris agent)
- Legal research and precedent synthesis (Rae agent)
- Timeline construction (Chronology agent)
- Precedent retrieval (Citation agent)
- Pattern matching (Casey agent)

**Humans own:**
- Case selection and strategy (Executive Director)
- Publication decisions (Executive Director + CEO)
- Attorney relationships (Executive Director)
- Research quality gates (Research Lead)
- Legal accuracy validation (external attorneys)
- Organizational governance (CEO + Legal Advisor)

**Result:** Agents work tirelessly on fact-finding. Humans apply judgment on questions that affect real people's lives.

## Key Decision Rights & Governance

### Case Publication Authority
- **Executive Director** proposes cases for publication
- **CEO** approves (final authority)
- **External attorneys** validate findings (gate before publication)
- **Impact:** Executive Director drives strategy; CEO maintains editorial control

### Engineering Authority
- **CTO** owns technical roadmap and architecture decisions
- **CTO** can approve <$50K spend without board approval
- **CTO** hires engineering team (with CEO sign-off on candidates)
- **Impact:** CTO has operational autonomy; escalates >$50K or strategic questions to CEO

### Program Authority
- **Executive Director** owns case selection and research priorities
- **Executive Director** approves agent findings for accuracy (Research Lead co-owns)
- **CEO** approves publication (final gate)
- **Impact:** Executive Director drives civil rights strategy; CEO ensures governance

### Board Authority
- Board approves hiring plan and compensation
- Board approves cases for publication if politically sensitive
- Board reviews quarterly progress (roadmap, case pipeline, financials)
- Board approves >$100K unbudgeted spend

## Key Decisions: Board Must Approve

Before executing this plan, the Board must decide:

### 1. Publication Authority
**Question:** Who approves published cases—CEO only, or can Executive Director approve with CEO visibility?

**Recommendation:** CEO final authority (maintains editorial control on public statements; Executive Director proposes and drives strategy).

### 2. Board Governance
**Question:** Should Executive Director of Civil Rights have a board seat, or advisory-only status?

**Recommendation:** Advisory initially (year-end review to elevate if needed). Board should include 1–2 civil rights advisors.

### 3. Investigative Lead Timing
**Question:** What's the trigger for hiring Investigative Lead in November—case volume, months in operation, or fixed date?

**Recommendation:** Fixed date (November 1) to have role ready when case intake volume picks up (anticipated September–October).

### 4. Legal Advisor Model
**Question:** In-house hire by Q1 2027, or ongoing contract?

**Recommendation:** Contract for Year 1 (~10 hrs/week, ~$50K annually). Upgrade to full-time if compliance/governance burden grows.

### 5. Hiring Contingencies
**Question:** If engineering hiring slips, should we adjust the scope of MCAS MVP?

**Recommendation:** Yes. If Backend Engineer not hired by Aug 15, defer to October and push MCAS MVP live to October 1 (impact: case research timeline slips 4–6 weeks).

### 6. Diversity & Inclusion
**Question:** What's our approach to recruiting diverse candidates, particularly for leadership roles?

**Recommendation:** Proactive sourcing from HBCUs, diversity-focused legal networks, civil rights organizations. Budget $15K–20K for executive search firm if needed.

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **CTO hire slips beyond June 30** | All engineering delayed 4–6 weeks | Interim technical advisor (contract CTO, 20 hrs/week) bridges gap |
| **Executive Director hire slips** | Case strategy delayed; attorney relationships slow | CEO leads case strategy directly until hire closes |
| **No suitable Backend Engineer by Aug 15** | MCAS MVP slips to October; case research delayed | CTO implements MCAS MVP alone; defer Backend hire to Q4 |
| **Agent/LLM Engineer unavailable** | Agent deployment pushed to Q1 2027 | Prioritize agent testing with attorneys; defer optimization to Q1 |
| **Budget overrun** | Runway impact; potential layoffs | Hiring contingency pool ($100K) + stretch goals (defer Comms/Legal hiring if needed) |
