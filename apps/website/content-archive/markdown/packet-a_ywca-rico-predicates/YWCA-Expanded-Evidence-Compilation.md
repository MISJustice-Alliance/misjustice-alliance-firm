## V. 2012-2016 DOJ INVESTIGATION: "RAPE CAPITAL" CONTEXT

### A. Federal Investigation Findings

**Source:** DOJ Civil Rights Division clearinghouse, Time Magazine (2014), Missoula book documentation

**Date:** May 1, 2012  
**Announcement:** Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights Thomas Perez

**Scope:**
- University of Montana Office of Public Safety
- Missoula Police Department
- Missoula County Attorney's Office (MCAO)

**Basis:**
> "In the past three years, there have been at least 80 reported rapes" resulting in national media describing Missoula as **"America's rape capital"**

#### Key Findings - Systematic Gender Bias

**From DOJ Investigation Report:**

1. **Discriminatory Prosecution Patterns**
   - Only **16% of sexual assault cases prosecuted** (2008-2012)
   - Of 350 sexual assaults reported to Missoula PD (January 2008-May 2012), "few were properly handled"
   - MCAO "declined to prosecute nearly every case of non-stranger assault involving an adult victim who was, at the time of the assault, subject to some type of heightened vulnerability – for example, in cases facilitated by drugs or alcohol"

2. **Institutional Failures**
   - "Substantial evidence suggesting that MCAO's response to allegations of sexual assault and rape discriminates against women"
   - "Discrimination is fueled, at least in part, by gender bias"
   - "Adult women victims, particularly victims of non-stranger sexual assault and rape, were often treated with disrespect, not informed of the status of their case and revictimized by the process"

3. **Training & Knowledge Deficiencies**
   - County Attorney did not provide Deputy County Attorneys with "basic knowledge and training about sexual assault necessary to effectively and impartially investigate and prosecute these cases"
   - "Police relied on stereotypes and myths about rape, rather than taking rape complaints seriously"

4. **Information Sharing Problems**
   - "Significant information-sharing problems among system partners"
   - "Interrupted or prohibited cross-agency sharing"
   - Pattern of institutional coordination failures
